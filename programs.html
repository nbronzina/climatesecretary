Programs

Our programs are designed to address the climate challenges faced by Madrid. Each initiative focuses on protecting residents, infrastructure, and the environment while fostering sustainability and resilience.

Cooling Corridors

Shaded walking routes with water mist systems to combat extreme heat.

Impact: Reduced urban walking temperatures by up to 3°C.

Beneficiaries: Pedestrians, outdoor workers, and elderly residents.

Emergency Cooling Shelters

Air-conditioned spaces accessible during heat alerts to protect vulnerable populations.

Locations: Available in all major districts. View the map.

Heatwave Early Alerts

Real-time notifications and advice to citizens during extreme heat events.

Status: Implemented citywide; 98% citizen engagement rate in 2030.

Urban Heat Island Reduction

Strategies to cool the city, including reflective materials and green roofs.

Future Plan: 50 additional green roofs by 2032.

Smart Water Monitoring

Digital tools to track and reduce water waste in households and public spaces.

Resources: Download the Smart Water App.

Rainwater Harvesting

Infrastructure to collect rainwater for irrigation and cleaning streets.

Implemented Areas: Southern Madrid and public parks.

Community Water Reserves

Local reservoirs to store water for critical use during droughts.

Status: 30% capacity reached in 2030.

Flood-Resistant Streets

Permeable pavements and enhanced drainage systems to prevent urban flooding.

Impact: Reduced flooding incidents in critical zones by 40% in 2029.

Stormwater Parks

Parks designed to absorb excess rainwater and reduce flood risks.

Beneficiaries: Communities near flood-prone areas.

Storm-Proof Housing

Subsidies for weather-resistant windows and roofs to withstand storms and hail.

Status: 1,500 households upgraded in 2030.

Urban Windbreaks

Tree barriers and structures to reduce wind impact in open areas.

Implemented Areas: Public squares and major parks.

Snow Removal Plans

Fast-response teams to clear snow and keep roads safe during rare snowfalls.

Contact: Call our snow removal hotline at 123-456-7890.

Winter Resilience Kits

Distribution of kits with salt, shovels, and emergency heating for extreme cold events.

Beneficiaries: Vulnerable households in high-altitude areas.

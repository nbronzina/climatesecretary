About Us

The Secretary of Climate Resilience is committed to protecting Madrid from extreme weather conditions and ensuring a sustainable future for all citizens.

Through clear strategies, innovation, and preparedness, we build a city resilient to the challenges of a changing climate.

Our Mission, Vision, and Values

Mission

To safeguard Madrid against climate risks while promoting innovative, sustainable, and inclusive solutions for a resilient city.

Vision

A future where Madrid is prepared for all climate challenges, thriving as a model city of resilience and sustainability.

Values

  • Inclusivity: No one is left behind in the fight against climate change.
  • Innovation: Using cutting-edge solutions to adapt and respond.
  • Transparency: Clear and open communication with citizens.
  • Preparedness: Anticipating and mitigating risks proactively.

Climate Risks in Madrid

Madrid faces various climate challenges that require immediate action and long-term planning. The most significant inclemencias climáticas include:

  • Olas de calor (Heatwaves): Frequent extreme heat events with temperatures exceeding 40°C, impacting health and energy systems.
  • Sequías prolongadas (Droughts): Water shortages caused by reduced rainfall, affecting agriculture, biodiversity, and water supply.
  • Inundaciones repentinas (Flash Floods): Intense rainfall during DANA episodes, overwhelming drainage systems and causing urban flooding.
  • Tormentas y granizo (Storms and Hail): Strong winds, hail, and thunderstorms that damage infrastructure, crops, and vehicles.
  • Nevadas extremas (Extreme Snowfall): Rare but severe events, such as Filomena, which disrupt transportation and daily life.
  • Vientos fuertes (Strong Winds): Gusts exceeding 70 km/h, causing falling objects, trees, and structural risks.

By understanding these risks, the Secretary can develop effective programs to protect residents and infrastructure.
